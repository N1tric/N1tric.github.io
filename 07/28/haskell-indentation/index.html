Indentations are used to make codes shorter and beginners confused.

Identations in Haskell are style-free, which means a tab is the same as a white space. In practical, people prefer to use space instead of tab because it’s easier to control and has a better view.

Identations with same spaces indicate a block.

a=1,b=2… are the members of let block. Each statement in the block must have the same identation, that is, have the same characters before them no matter whether the characters are space or not.
